Tribute to Swami Vivekananda

About Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda was born as Narendranath Datta on 12 January 1863 in Kolkata. He was a disciple of Ramakrishna Paramahamsa and played a key role in reviving Indian spirituality. He gained global fame after his powerful speech at the Parliament of World’s Religions in Chicago in 1893. He later founded the Ramakrishna Mission to promote education, service, and spiritual growth.

Achievements

  • Delivered the historic speech at the 1893 Chicago Parliament of Religions.
  • Popularized Hinduism and Indian philosophy globally
  • Founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897 for social and spiritual service
  • Introduced yoga and Vedanta to the Western world.
  • Inspired Indian youth with the call to "Arise, Awake, and Stop Not."
  • Authored famous works like Raja Yoga, Karma Yoga, and Jnana Yoga.
  • Influenced leaders of India\’s freedom movement with his thoughts and ideals.
